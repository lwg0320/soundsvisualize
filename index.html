Spotify genre fingerprints · Top 20 most popular genres

Every genre sounds like itself.

Across Spotify's 20 most popular genres, the same five audio traits — tempo, speechiness, danceability, energy, and loudness — land in completely different places. A genre's fingerprint is not just a style label; it's a measurable shape. No two genres share the same shape, and the gap between them is often larger than you'd expect.

Select any genre from the list to see its radar fingerprint — how it sits above or below the average across all genres on each trait.

Five traits that define a genre's sound

Tempo, speechiness, danceability, energy, and loudness are the five Spotify audio features that shape how a genre feels. Select a trait to read its definition, hear a low and high audio sketch, and explore the five real tracks with the highest and lowest values across all genres.

Page 2

Head-to-head genre comparison

The left side compares genre identity against the broader field. The right side shows what changes inside each genre when songs become more popular.

Genre fingerprint More or less than other genres

Each bar shows how a genre scores on that trait relative to the average across all genres, expressed as a z-score. A positive value means the genre sits above average — for example, a high Energy bar means songs in that genre are measurably more intense than most. A negative value means below average. This is about identity: what makes a genre sound like itself compared to everything else.

Popularity fingerprint Top third versus bottom third inside the genre

Each bar shows the difference in that trait between the most popular songs and the least popular songs within the same genre. A positive value means the genre's hits score higher on that trait than its deep cuts — for example, a high Danceability bar means popular songs in that genre are noticeably more danceable than obscure ones. This is about what pushes songs to the top inside a genre, not how the genre compares to others.

Game 1: match the fingerprint

A real Spotify track's audio fingerprint is shown as a radar chart — five traits mapped to z-scores. Three songs are listed below the radar. One of them is the exact track that generated the fingerprint. Read the shape and pick the right song. After each guess, all three fingerprints are revealed so you can compare.

Page 5

Game 2: find the closest neighbor

A source song is shown with its audio fingerprint. Two candidates are listed. Listen to all three, then pick whichever candidate's five-trait fingerprint sits closest to the source song. Euclidean distance across all five z-scored traits determines the right answer.

Every genre is different. Every song is different. Patterns still exist.

No two genres share the same fingerprint. No two songs within a genre are identical. But zoom out far enough and structure emerges — clusters of sound, shared tendencies, and traits that pull in the same direction across seemingly unrelated styles.
